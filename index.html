What went wrong with hypertext and by consequence most of modern media?

In short words?Everything :)

But first of all the links work only in one direction.

"Today's popular software simulates paper. The World Wide Web (another imitation of paper) trivialises our original hypertext model with one-way ever-breaking links and no management of version or contents."

This is a mission statement of Project Xanadu which was the first hypertext project, founded in 1960 by Ted Nelson. Administrators of Project Xanadu have declared it superior to the World Wide Web.

The core problem with today's media no matter the form are fake news selective and hard to find sources of information.




This is no different in newest advancements such as LLM GPT's which are known to hallucinate and not focused on fact checking.




The solution would be building a modular system with a base knowledge that could then be displayed to users in various formats. If you want to have a science article, clickbait or instagram caption it can be brought to you with a help of specialised GPTs. When we build this on a solid factual foundation all of these forms of transmitting knowledge could be coherent, interconnected. Soon we will be able to invent multi-modal communication tools. This will allow us to translate any input into any type of output. The translation layer will “intertwingle” the information into the knowledge base.
